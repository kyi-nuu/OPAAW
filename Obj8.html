8.

Women in Disasters

Overview

This one focuses on helping women during natural disasters, especially in rural areas. It calls for enhancing the capacity of institutions to prevent violence against women and ensuring women have access to safe zones, food, shelter, education, and healthcare. It also emphasizes the role of women in planning and responding to disasters.

Sub-Objectives

Enhancing capacities of governmental institutions and civil society organisations to prevent violence against women at the local, national and regional level.

Engaging with women’s civil society organisations in order to promote their effective empowerment to ensure access to their basic human rights.

Ensuring gender-based response to women and girl refugees, migrants, displaced, including those under foreign occupation, and facilitating their access to humanitarian safe zones, food, shelter, education, and healthcare in times of crisis.

Integrating sexual violence responses, including girl-child violence, in all humanitarian policies and strategies to prevent such violence, provide necessary assistance to victims and fight the impunity of perpetrators.

Facilitating women's full participation in the definition of a humanitarian response to natural disasters and crisis (assessment analysis and planning and implementation), and also in humanitarian actions, through national entities, civil society and/or community-based organisations.

Enhancing the role of religious leaders in the prevention of violence against women and girls.

Engaging men and boys in all steps of elimination of different forms of violence against women and girls.

Mobilising media and other communicative tools to combat violence against women and girls through the awareness-raising and sensitisation of society.

Quoted Objective: Women in Disasters: Providing humanitarian assistance for women and protecting them during natural disasters, particularly in rural areas.
